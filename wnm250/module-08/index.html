BRIEF HISTORY OF COMPUTER PROGRAMMING

  • The Antikythera mechanism is an ancient analog computer designed to predict astronomical positions and eclipses for calendrical and astrological purposes, as well as the Olympiads, the cycles of the ancient Olympic Games. (323BC - 31 BC)
  • 1936 Alan Turing invents the Turing machine, providing a formalisation of the concepts of algorithm and computation. The machine can be considered a model of a general purpose computer.
  • 1954 Fortran was invented, the first high level programming language to have a functional implementation.
  • 1976 Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak roll out the Apple 1, the 1st computer with a single circuit board.
  • 1990 Tim Berners-Lee develops HTML, giving rise to the World Wide Web.
  • The Digital Revolution (1950 - 1970), is the change from analog, mechanical, and electronic technology to digital technology. Central to this revolution is the mass production and widespread use of the computer, digital cellular phone, and the Internet.
